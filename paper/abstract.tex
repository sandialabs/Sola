This paper presents a software framework for performing principled computational outer-loop analyses in an approachable manner.
The framework consists of two complementary elements: \texttt{SABL}, a \texttt{MATLAB} library for rapid prototyping and solving small-to-modest problems, and \texttt{MrHyDE}, a \texttt{c++} library equipped with accessible high-performance computing capabilities.
Two full examples demonstrate how \texttt{SABL} as a high-level, mathematically intuitive library prepares users for the lower-level but more computationally powerful \texttt{MrHyDE} library.
A particular emphasis is given to PDE-constrained optimization and hyper-differential sensitivity analysis.
